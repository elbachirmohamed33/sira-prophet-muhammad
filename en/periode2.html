Socio-Religious Context

Date: 5th-6th century (500-610 CE)

Place: Arabian Peninsula (Hijaz, Najd, Yemen)

Key Figures: ʿAbd al-Muttalib (Prophet's grandfather), Waraqa ibn Nawfal (Hanif scholar)

Sources:

  • Ibn Hishâm (As-Sîra an-Nabawiyya)
  • Kitâb al-Aṣnâm by Ibn al-Kalbî (Arab idols)
  • Archaeological excavations of Qaryat al-Fâw

Summary: Polytheism: 360 idols at the Kaaba including Hubal (3m tall, red carnelian). Practices: Human sacrifices, burying daughters alive, star worship.

Main Lesson: Islam abolished barbaric practices while preserving noble values of honor and hospitality.

The Year of the Elephant (ʿÂm al-Fîl)

Date: 570 CE (month of Muharram)

Place: Mecca → Valley of Muhassar

Key Figures: Abraha al-Ashram (Himyarite king), ʿAbd al-Muttalib

Sources:

  • Quran 105 (Surah al-Fîl)
  • Inscription Ry 506 of King Abraha
  • Chronicle of John Malalas (Byzantium)

Summary: Forces: 60,000 men, 13 elephants. Miracle: Birds Abâbîl carrying stones of sijjîl, smallpox epidemic in the army.

Main Lesson: Allah protects His House (Kaaba) as a foreshadowing of the Prophet's ﷺ coming, born 50 days after this event.
