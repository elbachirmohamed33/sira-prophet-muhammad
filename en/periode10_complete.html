The Blessed Hijra

622 CE - 1 AH

The Beginning of Islamic History

The Sacred Journey to Yathrib

üó°Ô∏è The Plot of Dar al-Nadwa - The Night of Assassination

On the night of 26 Safar in the first year of Hijra (622 CE), the Quraysh gathered at Dar al-Nadwa to devise a final plan to eliminate Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫. The meeting was attended by leaders from all Quraysh tribes, and after lengthy consultations, Abu Jahl proposed a cunning plan: to select a strong young man from each tribe to strike the Prophet Ô∑∫ with one blow, thus scattering his blood among the tribes so that Banu Hashim could not fight all of Quraysh.

The Quraysh agreed to this vile plan and selected eleven men from among the nobles of Quraysh, including: Abu Jahl ibn Hisham, Abu Lahab, Al-Hakam ibn Abi al-As, Al-Aqra ibn Habis, Zam'a ibn al-Aswad, Tu'aima ibn Adi, Abu al-Bakhtari, Nawfal ibn Khuwaylid, Manbah and Nabih the sons of al-Hajjaj, and Umayya ibn Khalaf.

üë§ Ali Sleeps in the Prophet's Ô∑∫ Bed

When Angel Jibril Ô∑∫ informed Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ of the Quraysh plot, Allah commanded him not to sleep in his bed that night. The Prophet Ô∑∫ then asked his cousin Ali ibn Abi Talib Ô∑∫ to sleep in his bed and cover himself with his green Hadhrami cloak. He told him: "Sleep in my bed and wrap yourself in this green Hadhrami cloak, sleep in it, for nothing harmful will reach you from them."

Ali Ô∑∫ courageously accepted this dangerous mission, knowing he could be killed. When the polytheists came and stood at the door waiting, Ali was sleeping in the Prophet's Ô∑∫ bed covered with his cloak, and they thought the Prophet Ô∑∫ was sleeping in his bed.

"And [remember, O Muhammad], when those who disbelieved plotted against you to restrain you or kill you or evict you [from Mecca]. But they plan, and Allah plans. And Allah is the best of planners."
Quran 8:30

üåô The Prophet's Ô∑∫ Exit from Among the Besiegers

On that blessed night, Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ left his house while the polytheists surrounded it, and Allah blinded them so they could not see him. The Prophet Ô∑∫ took a handful of dust and threw it on their heads while reciting Allah's words: "And We have put before them a barrier and behind them a barrier and covered them, so they do not see."

Then the Prophet Ô∑∫ went to the house of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq Ô∑∫ at an unusual time. When Abu Bakr saw him, he said: "What brings you, O Messenger of Allah, at this hour?" The Prophet Ô∑∫ said: "Have your people leave." Abu Bakr said: "They are only your family, may my father and mother be sacrificed for you, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "Allah has given me permission to leave and migrate." Abu Bakr said: "Companionship, may my father and mother be sacrificed for you, O Messenger of Allah." The Prophet Ô∑∫ said: "Companionship."

üê™ Preparation for the Journey

Preparing the Mounts

Abu Bakr had prepared two camels for months, feeding them acacia leaves in preparation for this blessed day.

Path Guide

Abu Bakr hired Abdullah ibn Urayqit al-Laythi, who was a polytheist but trustworthy, to be their guide.

Provisions Preparation

Asma bint Abi Bakr prepared food and water, while Abdullah ibn Abi Bakr was tasked with collecting Quraysh news and reporting it to them.

üï≥Ô∏è In the Cave of Thawr - Three Blessed Nights

Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ and Abu Bakr headed to the cave of Thawr on Mount Thawr south of Mecca, in the opposite direction from the Medina route, to mislead the pursuers. They reached the cave at night, Abu Bakr entered first to ensure the safety of the place, then the Prophet Ô∑∫ entered.

In the cave, many miracles occurred demonstrating Allah's protection of His messenger Ô∑∫. A spider wove its web at the cave entrance in a short time, and pigeons came to lay eggs at the entrance, making the pursuers think that no one had entered the cave for a long time.

"Anas Ô∑∫ reported that Abu Bakr as-Siddiq told him: 'I said to the Prophet Ô∑∫ while we were in the cave: If one of them looked under his feet, he would see us.' He said: 'What do you think of two whose third is Allah?'"
Sahih al-Bukhari and Muslim

üîç Intense Pursuit by Quraysh

When the Quraysh discovered that the Prophet Ô∑∫ had escaped, they announced a reward of one hundred camels for whoever brought them back alive or dead. Pursuers spread out on all roads leading to Medina, and some came right to the cave door, but Allah turned them away from it.

Among the pursuers was Suraqa ibn Malik al-Mudliji, who coveted the reward and followed them on his horse. But every time he approached them, his horse's legs sank into the ground, until he understood this was Allah's command, so he asked them for safety and promised to turn people away from them.

She brought them food and water every night, and conveyed important news to them.

Abu Bakr's freedman, he herded sheep during the day and passed by the cave to provide them with milk, erasing footprints with his sheep.

"If you do not aid the Prophet - Allah has already aided him when those who disbelieved had driven him out [of Mecca] as one of two, when they were in the cave and he said to his companion, 'Do not grieve; indeed Allah is with us.'"
Quran 9:40

üê´ Departure Toward Medina

After three nights in the cave, Abdullah ibn Urayqit brought them the camels at their appointed time. They left the cave by an unusual path, heading toward the coast then east toward Medina, avoiding the usual routes monitored by Quraysh.

They took a difficult route along the western coast, passing by places like that of Umm Ma'bad, where the miracle of the goat occurred - it gave milk after being lean and milkless, when the Prophet Ô∑∫ touched its udder and invoked Allah.

üåÖ Arrival at Medina the Illuminated

On Monday, 12 Rabi' al-Awwal of the first year of Hijra (September 24, 622 CE), Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ arrived at Medina the Illuminated. The people of Medina had been eagerly awaiting him, and when they saw him, they celebrated and came out to welcome him.

The Prophet Ô∑∫ first stopped at Quba at Kulthum ibn al-Hadm's place, staying there four days during which he built the Quba Mosque, the first mosque built in Islam. Then he entered Medina on Friday, and prayed Friday in the valley with the Muslims.

"Al-Bara ibn Azib said: 'I never saw the people of Medina rejoice over anything like they rejoiced over the Messenger of Allah Ô∑∫, to the point that the maidservants were saying: The Messenger of Allah has come, the Messenger of Allah has come!'"
Sahih al-Bukhari

üìÖ The Beginning of Islamic History

With the blessed prophetic Hijra, official Islamic history began. Muslims took this great event as the beginning of the Hijri calendar, recognizing its importance in Islamic history. It was not merely a geographical move, but a complete civilizational and spiritual transformation.

The Hijra taught Muslims that jihad in Allah's path includes sacrificing homeland, family, and wealth for religion, and that Allah helps those who help Him regardless of difficult circumstances. It also showed the importance of planning and taking means while relying on Allah.
